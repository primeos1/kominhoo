## Design Context

### Users
Working Nigerian women aged 25–40, results-driven and willing to invest in quality skincare. They browse on mobile during commutes or lunch breaks. They are familiar with beauty trends but may be new to K-beauty specifics. They want to feel pampered and seen — not talked down to.

### Brand Personality
**Luxurious · Feminine · Aspirational**
Kominhoo should feel like walking into a high-end Lagos boutique that happens to stock the best of Seoul. Confident, editorial, culturally rooted — not generic beauty-brand beige.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Soft luxury: editorial, organic, warm.**
Like a high-end beauty magazine spread — think *Vogue Africa* meets K-beauty catalog. The color palette (lily-inspired) drives everything: deep rose (#893941), soft blush (#CB7885), warm chartreuse (#D4D994), olive (#5E6623). Light mode. Generous white space. Full-bleed photography.

**Visual references**: Campaign-style portraits of diverse African women with glowing skin. Layered typographic hierarchy. Unhurried, confident pacing.

**Anti-references**: Pastel generic e-commerce, neon green "tech" vibes, generic Shopify templates.

### Typography
- Display / headlines: **Bodoni Moda** — editorial fashion, feminine authority
- Body / UI: **Jost** — geometric but warm, readable at small sizes

### Design Principles
1. **Portraiture first** — people sell skincare, not bottles. Hero is always campaign portraits.
2. **Rose is the new black** — #893941 replaces dark/black as the primary anchor color. Chartreuse (#D4D994) is the accent, used sparingly.
3. **Editorial rhythm** — vary section backgrounds (cream, white, rose, rose-dark) to create a magazine-like scroll.
4. **No generic card grids** — vary card sizes, aspect ratios, and layouts to break monotony.
5. **Mobile portrait-first** — 95%+ of Nigerian women shop on phones. Every layout must be designed for a vertical phone screen first.
